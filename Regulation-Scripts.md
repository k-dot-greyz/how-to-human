### **regulation_scripts.md**

*A library of scripts for managing runaway processes and system instability.*

#### **1. Hard Reboot (cold_shock.sh)**

* **Purpose:** To trigger a non-graceful system reboot when processes are hung and unresponsive. This is the equivalent of holding down the power button. It forcefully terminates all running user-space applications (like circular thoughts) and reloads the kernel.  
* **Execution:**  
  1. Apply a sudden, intense cold stimulus. The most effective methods are a cold shower or submerging your face in a bowl of ice water.  
  2. The system will trigger a mammalian dive reflex, a low-level firmware response that immediately drops heart rate and reroutes resources to core functions.  
  3. This process is jarring but extremely effective at breaking out of catastrophic software loops. Use when standard debugging fails.

#### **2. Sync System Clock (box_breathing.sh)**

* **Purpose:** To manually regulate the system's clock cycle when it's running too fast (panic) or erratically (anxiety). This script forces the I/O (breathing) into a stable, predictable rhythm, which in turn stabilizes the CPU (your brain).  
* **Execution:**  
  1. inhale(4): Inhale slowly for a count of 4 seconds.  
  2. hold(4): Hold the breath for a count of 4 seconds.  
  3. exhale(4): Exhale slowly for a count of 4 seconds.  
  4. wait(4): Wait for a count of 4 seconds before the next cycle.  
  5. loop: Repeat the cycle for 1-2 minutes. The rigid, four-sided structure ("box") gives the CPU a simple, predictable clock to sync with, overriding the chaotic signals of panic.

#### **3. Poll External Sensors (54321_grounding.sh)**

* **Purpose:** To force the OS to stop processing a runaway internal loop (anxiety, flashbacks, dissociation) and re-read data from all attached physical peripherals. This is a diagnostic script to re-establish a connection with the external environment.  
* **Execution:**  
  1. Force a read from the optical sensors: Name **5** things you can see.  
  2. Force a read from the tactile sensors: Name **4** things you can feel.  
  3. Force a read from the audio sensors: Name **3** things you can hear.  
  4. Force a read from the olfactory sensors: Name **2** things you can smell.  
  5. Force a read from the gustatory sensors: Name **1** thing you can taste.  
  6. By sequentially polling each input, you force the system to allocate resources away from the internal error state and back to processing the present reality.
